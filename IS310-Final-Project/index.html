Cultural Analysis of IPL 2021 Through Twitter Data

Introduction

The Indian Premier League (IPL) is not just a cricketing event; it's a cultural phenomenon that captivates millions around the globe. In this project, we set out to explore the cultural conversations surrounding IPL 2021 by analyzing Twitter data. Our goal is to uncover patterns and sentiments that reflect the event's impact on the public and how technology mediates the discourse around it. The IPL coincides with a significant presence on social media, especially on platforms like Twitter where fans, teams, and pundits share their thoughts and feelings. As such, IPL-related tweets offer a rich dataset to examine the cultural aspects of this sporting event. We aim to see how the discussions evolved over the season, the geographical diversity of IPL's audience, and the overall sentiment attached to the event. During the project, pivots were necessary to accommodate the challenges posed by data collection and to align with the objectives of the course, which focuses on cultural data and its computational analysis. For instance, we refined our data collection methods to capture a more accurate representation of the global IPL discourse.

Data Collection & Curation

To harness the breadth of discussions, we gathered tweets using the hashtag #ipl2021 during the tournament season. The data collection involved using Twitter's API to fetch relevant tweets and metadata, followed by a curation process that ensured data quality and relevance. Here's how the data collection and curation unfolded: Utilized Twitter API v2 to fetch tweets containing #ipl2021. Implemented data cleaning techniques to process and standardize user locations for geographical analysis. Applied named entity recognition to extract mentions of players, teams, and other IPL-related keywords. Conclusion & Reflection

This project illuminates the digital cultural landscape of IPL 2021, showcasing the diversity and depth of the tournament's discourse on Twitter. It highlights how digital platforms serve as a barometer for public sentiment and engagement, reflecting both the celebratory spirit and critical discussions around the event. We encountered challenges, particularly with the variability of user-generated content on Twitter. Through iterative data cleaning and refinement of analysis techniques, these obstacles were navigated successfully. Potential future directions for this research include a longitudinal study spanning multiple IPL seasons to track cultural shifts over time. Additionally, a comparative analysis with other sports events could provide further insights into the unique cultural footprint of IPL. For researchers interested in expanding upon this work, the dataset and methods are documented comprehensively to facilitate reproducibility and further exploration.
